This is the page for the user guide.

Reef Mates integrates digital calendars from the 2 most popular calendar platforms, Google Calendar and Apple/iCalendar. Both of these platforms allow users to keep a private calendar that they can share read-only access to through a secret URL.


This means your calendar is only viewable to you and others you trust to share this secret URL with. We encourage users to follow the provided tutorial for their preferred online calendar platform to find their secret URL. These tutorials do not include account creation for either platform.


After adding a calendar to your profile, you can invite other family members to your events using one of the methods described in the "Inviting People" guide below.

Inviting People

Google calendar

1. Create a new calendar

If you already have a calendar set up that you want to use for this purpose, please skip to step 2.

Open google calendar on desktop, in the left column there is a collection of “My Calendars”. To make a new calendar, click the + sign next to “Other Calendars”, then select “Create new Calendar”. You can name the calendar anything, then select “Create Calendar”. Afterwards, click the back arrow, and your newly created calendar should appear under “My Calendars”.


2. Finding the secret URL

Click the 3 dots beside your calendar, then select “Settings and Sharing”.

Scroll down to the “Integrate Calendar” section, this has a censored URL labelled “Secret address in iCal format”. Click the copy button on the right side to copy the URL to your clipboard.

Google might provide a security warning telling you to only give this URL to people you trust. Now you can paste this URL in the URL section of the form when adding a new reef member to the Home Reef.


Apple calendar

1. Create a new calendar

For an Apple Calendar to be shareable it must be on iCloud, so ensure the Calendars app is enabled in your iCloud settings.

If you already have a calendar you wish to use of this purpose, please skip to step 2.

In the Apple calendar desktop app, click File, then “New Calendar”. You can name your new calendar anything, it should appear on the left-hand side along with any other calendars you have.


2. Finding the secret URL

When you hover over your calendar, a little person icon should appear, click that, the select “Share Calendar”.

Then you will see a checkbox next to the words “Public Calendar”, check this box, then click Done. Although it says “Allow anyone to subscribe to a read-only version of this calendar”, the only people able to subscribe are those who you share a link with.

When you hover over the calendar and click the person icon again, a URL will now appear. After copying the URL, you can paste it in the URL section of the form when adding a new reef member to the Home Reef.


Inviting Other Family Members to your Events

Method 1: Inviting by name

Any family member can be invited to an event by including their name in the first line of the event description/notes. If you wish to invite multiple family members, just separate their names by commas. It is important when adding someone this way that you spell their name the same way it appears on their profile on the Home Reef, including capitalisation and punctuation, otherwise they won't be recognised as invited.

Method 2: Inviting by email

This method can only be used to invite family members with an email address attached to their profile. Type the family members' email address into “Add guests” on Google calendar, or “Add Invitees” on Apple calendar.

In Google calendar you have the option to send an email to this person to let them know they have been invited to your event. If the email address is the one associated with their google account (if they have one), the event is automatically added to their calendar as well.

On Apple calendar the email invitation automatically happens. The event will only appear on the invitee's calendar if the address used is the @icloud.com address associated with their apple account (if they have one).

Google and Apple calendar users can still invite each other to events by email, they just won't appear automatically on the invitees calendar unless they use the same platform.
